WHAT IS A HACK4FARMING?

Hack4Farming is a series of agriculture data-themed hackathons held in the developing world. Hack4Farming grew out of my shared experiences working with agricultural development organizations in Africa on capacity building. While most were committed to implementing more advanced analytics technologies in their work, and saw opportunity for the climate data available to them, restrictions on time and barriers in computer science limited the amount that could be accomplished.

Hack4Farming was conceived as a new type of capacity building event for this audience. The coding and developer communities in Africa are vibrant and fast-growing, and are an ideal resource for African organizations hoping to overcome data-related challenges and augment their analytics. Hosting a hackathon is a well-known method of bringing together diverse groups of professionals and skilled students to "hack" a common problem. In the course of doing so, participants collaborate, share ideas, and learn about each others' skills. While prizes for the best hack are common incentives, the true value of a hackathon is often in the connections made and new techniques shared.

While hackathons are extremely common in the software development world, my surveys of the intended agriculture development audience showed most did not know what a hackathon was and had never attended one before.
