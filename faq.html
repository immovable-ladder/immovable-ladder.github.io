Frequently Asked Questions

by aWhere

Updated on June 1, 2015

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is traditionally an event bringing together computer programmers, graphic designers, software developers, and other persons with technical skills to collaborate intensively on projects. The events usually last 1-2 days, and participants usually work in teams and compete to develop the best solutions to pre-set challenges.

More recently, international development organizations have been using hackathons as opportunities for intensive brainstorming, without necessarily featuring coding or software development.

Is Hack Kilimo focused on coding only?

Hack Kilimo is primarily focused on bringing agriculture development organizations from across East Africa together to hack a common data challenge, so the solutions need not be strictly technology-based. However, we believe the East African technology community has an extremely important role to play in developing regional agriculture, and we hope the involvement of African coders will help the organizations to develop better and more efficient solutions to their challenges.

Who can participate in the Hackathon?

Software programmers, developers, and other persons with computer and data management expertise who share a passion for helping develop East African agriculture.

Is there a fee to participate?

There is no fee to participate, but prospective hackers must contact the organizers in advance to be assigned to a team. In addition, the participant must cover their own travel to the venue in Nairobi.

Do winners receive prizes?

Yes, there will be a cash prize for the overall winner of the hackathon, as well as additional smaller prizes for projects that meet certain criteria.

What is the challenge?

The specific challenge will be announced at the start of the Hackathon. However, the challenge will be on the general theme of agricultural data management and communication in rural African conditions.

Do I need to be a hacker, programmer, or computer expert to participate?

Not at all! Anyone may attend the kick-off networking event or the presentations of final projects. Further, while we give preference to persons with technical skills in assigning the hacking teams, if you feel you have particular expertise in agriculture, communications, or other fields that would be helpful on a team, get in contact with us.
